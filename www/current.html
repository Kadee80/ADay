DID YOU KNOW

Currents are the internal movement of the water sometimes described as the push and pull in the river. The current moving down river towards the bay is called the ebb, if it moves upstream it is called the flood.

Do not confuse the direction of waves with the current - they are different. On a windy day the current and the waves can move in opposite directions.

PROCEDURE

  • Every 30 Minutes toss a stick or orange into the river and note the direction of travel.
  • Record ebb tide, flood tide, or slack tide if there is no movement.
  • Recover the object for later use. Do not litter.
  • To calculate speed use a measuring tape and a stopwatch.
  • Have one student line up with the floating objects starting point.
  • After the object floats for 60 seconds, line up a second student with that point.
  • Calculate the distance per second by dividing total distance by 60 seconds
